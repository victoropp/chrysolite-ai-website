# Acceptable Use Policy

**Document ID:** ISMS-POL-13
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-03-02
**Review Date:** 2027-03-02
**Owner:** CISO / HR
**Approved By:** CEO
**Classification:** Internal — Confidential

---

## 1. Purpose

To define acceptable and unacceptable use of Chrysolite AI's information assets, systems, networks, and devices, ensuring they are used in a manner that protects the company, its customers, and its employees. Aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A controls A.5.10, A.6.7, and A.8.1.

---

## 2. Scope

All Chrysolite AI employees, contractors, and third parties who use Chrysolite AI assets, systems, or networks, whether on company premises, remotely, or via personal devices (BYOD).

---

## 3. General Principles

Chrysolite AI assets are provided primarily for business use. Incidental personal use is permitted where it:

- Does not compromise security, performance, or availability
- Does not involve illegal activities
- Does not consume excessive resources
- Does not create legal liability for the company

When using company assets, users must remember that **all activity on company systems may be monitored and is subject to this policy**.

---

## 4. Acceptable Use

### 4.1 Internet and Web

**Permitted:**
- Business research and reference
- Accessing cloud services required for work
- Incidental personal browsing during breaks
- Professional development and learning resources

**Not Permitted:**
- Visiting sites hosting illegal content (copyright infringement, CSAM, hacking tools, etc.)
- Streaming video/audio for personal entertainment during work hours in ways that consume significant bandwidth
- Bypassing web filtering controls (VPN to circumvent company filtering)
- Conducting personal business ventures using company resources

### 4.2 Email

**Permitted:**
- Business communications using the @chrysoliteai.com email account
- Incidental personal email (non-sensitive) using the company account

**Not Permitted:**
- Sending customer personal data to personal email accounts
- Sending sensitive company information to unapproved external recipients
- Forwarding company emails to personal accounts automatically
- Phishing, spamming, or impersonating others
- Using company email for political activity, chain letters, or unsolicited commercial messaging

### 4.3 Software and Applications

**Permitted:**
- Software approved and listed in the IT-approved software list
- Software downloaded from official, verified sources
- Open-source software that has been reviewed for security and licence compatibility

**Not Permitted:**
- Installing unlicensed, cracked, or pirated software
- Installing software that has not been approved by IT without prior written approval
- Disabling security software (EDR, firewall, MDM) without CISO approval
- Introducing software from unknown or untrusted sources

### 4.4 Data Handling

**Permitted:**
- Accessing and processing data required for your role
- Storing work data in approved, secure locations (company cloud storage)

**Not Permitted:**
- Copying customer data to personal devices without explicit authorisation
- Processing customer personal data for any purpose other than the stated business purpose
- Using customer data to train personal or third-party AI/ML systems
- Storing sensitive company data in unapproved cloud services (personal Dropbox, Google Drive personal, etc.)

### 4.5 Social Media

**Permitted:**
- Personal social media use that does not involve Chrysolite AI confidential information
- Sharing approved, public Chrysolite AI marketing content
- Identifying as a Chrysolite AI employee on professional networks (LinkedIn)

**Not Permitted:**
- Posting confidential information, customer names, financial data, or unreleased product information
- Making statements that could be misconstrued as official Chrysolite AI positions without approval
- Disparaging customers, competitors, or colleagues publicly in ways that could harm the company

---

## 5. Remote Working

When working remotely:

- Company devices must be used for processing confidential data (personal devices for non-sensitive work only, and only with MDM enrolled BYOD)
- Public Wi-Fi must not be used without a full-tunnel VPN
- Screen must not be visible to family members, flatmates, or public when processing sensitive information
- Work calls discussing confidential matters should be taken in a private space

---

## 6. Monitoring

Chrysolite AI reserves the right to monitor activity on its systems and networks, including:

- Network traffic (content filtering, anomaly detection)
- Email metadata and content where legally permitted and proportionate
- Access logs, authentication events, and system activity

Monitoring is conducted for security, operational, and compliance purposes. Employees are informed of monitoring through this policy and their employment contract. Monitoring is conducted in compliance with applicable employment law and GDPR.

---

## 7. Reporting Violations

If you observe a violation of this policy by a colleague, you should report it to your line manager or the CISO. Reports can be made anonymously via the designated reporting channel (details in employee handbook). Retaliation against individuals who make good-faith reports is prohibited.

---

## 8. Consequences

Violations of this policy will be handled in accordance with the disciplinary procedures set out in the Human Resources Security Policy (ISMS-POL-12). Serious violations may result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.

---

## 9. Acknowledgement

All employees and contractors must sign an acknowledgement confirming they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this policy. This acknowledgement is obtained at onboarding and each time the policy is materially updated.

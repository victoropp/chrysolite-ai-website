# Physical & Environmental Security Policy

**Document ID:** ISMS-POL-05
**Version:** 1.0
**Effective Date:** 2026-03-02
**Review Date:** 2027-03-02
**Owner:** CISO / Office Manager
**Approved By:** CEO
**Classification:** Internal — Confidential

---

## 1. Purpose

To prevent unauthorised physical access, damage, and interference to Chrysolite AI's information assets, people, and facilities. Aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A controls A.7.1–A.7.14.

---

## 2. Scope

All Chrysolite AI office premises, server rooms, co-location facilities, home offices used for remote work, and any location where Chrysolite AI equipment or information is accessed or stored.

---

## 3. Physical Security Perimeters

### 3.1 Office Premises

- All entrances to Chrysolite AI office premises must be secured and access-controlled
- Visitors must sign in and out at reception; visitor logs retained for 12 months
- Visitors must be escorted at all times in areas containing information systems or sensitive information
- "Tailgating" (piggybacking through access-controlled doors) is prohibited and must be challenged

### 3.2 Secure Areas

Areas containing critical infrastructure (servers, network equipment, security systems) are designated **Secure Areas** and require:

- Electronic access control (key card, PIN, or biometric)
- Access restricted to named, authorised personnel only
- CCTV coverage of entry/exit points where legally permissible
- Access logs reviewed monthly by CISO

### 3.3 Clear Desk and Clear Screen Policy

- Employees must not leave sensitive information visible on their desk when away, even briefly
- Computer screens must be locked when unattended (automatic lock after 5 minutes; immediate lock: Win+L / Cmd+Ctrl+Q)
- Printed documents containing sensitive information must be collected immediately from shared printers
- Sensitive documents must be filed securely or shredded when no longer needed

---

## 4. Physical Access Controls

### 4.1 Access Provisioning

- Physical access credentials (key cards, keys, PIN codes) are issued by the Office Manager with CISO approval for secure areas
- Access rights are recorded and reviewed quarterly

### 4.2 Access Revocation

On termination or role change, physical access credentials must be returned and/or deactivated on the last day. HR is responsible for notifying the Office Manager immediately.

### 4.3 Lost or Stolen Credentials

Lost or stolen physical access credentials must be reported to the Office Manager and CISO immediately. Physical access systems must be updated (credential invalidated) within 2 hours of the report.

---

## 5. Equipment Security

### 5.1 Equipment Siting

- Equipment must be positioned to minimise the risk of:
  - Shoulder surfing (screens visible to unauthorised parties)
  - Environmental hazards (proximity to water sources, direct sunlight, heat sources)
  - Physical theft (unattended in public spaces)

### 5.2 Equipment Outside Premises

Company equipment taken outside the office (laptops, phones, USB drives) must be:

- Carried as hand luggage on public transport; never left unattended in vehicles
- Kept in a bag or case when not in use in public
- Password-protected and encrypted at all times
- Reported to IT immediately if lost or stolen (see Incident Management Policy ISMS-POL-09)

### 5.3 Unattended Equipment

Equipment in shared spaces (meeting rooms, hot desks) must be secured to a fixed object with a physical cable lock when left unattended for more than 5 minutes.

---

## 6. Cabling Security

- Network and power cabling must be protected from unauthorised interception or damage
- External network connections must terminate at a controlled demarcation point
- Cabling in publicly accessible areas must be concealed and protected

---

## 7. Environmental Threats

### 7.1 Power

- UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) must protect all critical on-premises server infrastructure
- Redundant power feeds where technically feasible

### 7.2 Temperature and Humidity

- Server rooms must maintain temperature between 18–27°C and relative humidity between 40–60%
- Temperature/humidity monitoring with automated alerts

### 7.3 Fire and Flood

- Fire suppression systems (gaseous or suppression system; not water-based in server rooms)
- Equipment must not be sited below water or drainage pipes where avoidable

---

## 8. Remote Working

Employees working remotely are responsible for ensuring:

- Their home working environment provides adequate physical security (screen not visible to third parties, visitors unable to access equipment)
- Company equipment is secured when not in use
- Sensitive printed documents are stored securely and shredded when no longer needed
- Any physical security incident (theft, loss of equipment) is reported immediately
